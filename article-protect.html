Listen. Act. Protect – Understanding POCSO and Building Safer Futures

The **Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012** is a comprehensive law enacted in India to safeguard children from sexual assault, harassment, and pornography. It is one of the strongest legal frameworks in the world to protect children from abuse.

Every child under 18 is covered under POCSO. The law ensures **child-friendly reporting, investigation, and trial procedures** so survivors are not re-traumatized.

Why Listen?

Children often give **signals** when they feel unsafe. Listening carefully means noticing changes in behavior, emotions, or fears. A child who feels heard is more likely to come forward and seek help.

How to Act?

Taking action is crucial. Under POCSO, **every individual is legally bound to report cases** of child sexual abuse. Failure to do so can lead to penalties. Parents, teachers, and citizens must not ignore warning signs.

Step 1: Report Immediately

Call **1098 (CHILDLINE)** or file a complaint with the nearest police station. POCSO makes reporting mandatory and protects the identity of the child.

Step 2: Legal Protection

The child’s statement must be recorded in a **safe and friendly environment**, preferably by a female officer. The Act ensures quick trials through Special Courts.

Step 3: Emotional Support

Children need **counseling, therapy, and reassurance**. NGOs and Child Welfare Committees provide psychological support throughout the process.

Why Protect?

Protecting means creating a world where children feel safe — at home, in school, and online. POCSO empowers society to act collectively against abuse and ensures **zero tolerance** towards exploitation.

"Listening with empathy, acting with courage, and protecting with determination is the true spirit of POCSO."

Helplines & Resources

  • CHILDLINE 1098 – 24/7 emergency helpline for children in distress.
  • National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR) – ncpcr.gov.in
  • POCSO e-Box – Online tool to report child abuse: pocsoebox.gov.in
