Empowering a Safer Future for Every Child

Shield 360 is a program dedicated to creating awareness about the *Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012*. Our mission is to empower children, parents, and educators with the knowledge and tools to ensure a safer environment for every child. We believe that awareness is the first and most crucial step towards prevention.

How Educators Can Help

1. Educators and Informers

Teach students about good touch and bad touch in an age-appropriate way. Spread awareness about childrenâ€™s rights and protections under the POCSO Act. Educate children on how to say NO and whom to approach if they feel unsafe.

2. Observers and Identifiers

Be alert to behavioral or physical changes in children that may signal abuse. Identify and respond to signs of trauma, fear, or withdrawal. Notice unusual interactions between children and adults or peers.

3. Supporters and Caregivers

Create a safe and trusting classroom environment so children feel comfortable sharing. Provide emotional support to children who disclose sensitive issues. Avoid blaming or shaming the child and instead reassure them.

4. Mandatory Reporters

Teachers are legally bound to report any suspicion or knowledge of sexual offences against children under the POCSO Act. Ensure proper documentation and immediately inform the school authorities, Child Welfare Committee, or police.

5. Collaborators with Parents and Community

Conduct awareness sessions for parents about POCSO and child safety. Work with NGOs, child protection cells, and local authorities for awareness programs. Encourage community vigilance for child safety outside school.

Our Core Pillars

Awareness & Education:

Providing workshops and resources on POCSO Act provisions, child safety, and recognizing signs of abuse.

Empowerment:

Teaching children about their rights and how to seek help from trusted adults or official channels.

Safe Spaces:

Encouraging the creation of a supportive and open dialogue within families, schools, and communities.

Reporting & Support:

Guiding individuals on the correct procedures for reporting incidents and offering support to survivors.

  • Learning more about the POCSO Act and its importance.
  • Being a vigilant and trustworthy adult for the children in your life.
